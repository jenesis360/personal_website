About

I am a doctoral student at the University of Oxford studying the most fundamental questions of particle physics. Since July 2016, I have been based at CERN in Geneva, Switzerland.

As a member of the ATLAS Experiment, I collaborate with scientists from around the world to look for evidence of new particles made at the Large Hadron Collider. Supervised by Alan Barr and John March-Russell, much of my research sits at the exciting interface of theory and experiment, understanding how to discover supersymmetry and dark matter.

Originally from London, England, I subsequently went to the University of Oxford for my undergraduate studies. I then spent a year in Canada at the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics where I was supervised by Natalia Toro (now at SLAC, Stanford University) and received my MSc from the University of Waterloo.

I enjoy doing science outreach, travelling, classical music and graphic design. As an undergraduate, I designed websites and played the violin for numerous music ensembles. I have grapheme-colour synaesthesia, where my brain associates colours to letters and numbers.
