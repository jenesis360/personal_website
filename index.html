Research

Particle physics

What are fundamental building blocks of our universe? How do these subatomic constituents interact? These are the questions particle physicists address every day.

Supersymmetry and dark matter

We know there are new laws of physics awaiting discovery but what could they be? Theory suggests there is a 'mirror world' of supersymmetric particles. Astrophysics argues there are new 'dark' particles that make up 80% of the matter in our universe. Only experiment can reveal their nature.

ATLAS experiment

The ATLAS experiment essentially captures images of proton-proton collisions, with thousands of scientists working together to ensure data taking and analyses are possible at the energy frontier.

Continuous spin particles

My master's project studied the field theory for so-called continuous spin particles. These remain theoretically mysterious but could deepen our understanding of gravity and electromagnetism, the two long-range forces of nature.
